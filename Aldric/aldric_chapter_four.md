# Aldric — Chapter Four
## A Chronicle of the Central Plains

---

## Prologue

*The watch post fire burns low. Root sits beside it, still, ancient, watching the flames with the attention of something that has not seen fire in four thousand years and finds it neither threatening nor comforting but simply — present.*

*Edwick writes.*

*You look at the road south and think about five days and the shape of what needs to happen inside them.*

---

## Part One: The Rememberers

The road south from the watch post. Root two lengths behind. Edwick behind Root. Fera behind Edwick. The plains opening around you and the army three and a half days from marching and the Lord Commander two days south of Greyvane.

At midday: a column on the road. Moving north. Not soldiers. Not refugees — refugees move south.

Orcs.

Twenty-five, perhaps. A warband moving with the loose purposeful formation of people who know where they're going. At the front, a figure that carries itself differently — the quality of deliberate stillness that moves with them rather than stopping them.

A **Rememberer**.

The warband stops. You stop. The Rememberer looks at Root. Root looks at the Rememberer.

Something passes between them in the silence that has nothing to do with language.

You wait. You let the reading complete.

The Rememberer speaks first, to Root, in the Common Tongue.

> *"You came from the north. From the boreal forests."*
>
> *"Yes."*
>
> *"You are maintaining something. My people have felt it for eight hundred years — the pressure from above. You are the source of that pressure."*
>
> *"Part of it. The outer layer. The inner layer was the network."*
>
> *"The hollow points. We called them the old wounds. The places where the world was hurt and never healed."*
>
> *"They are not wounds. They are seals."*
>
> *"Yes. We know that now. We have known it for three days."*

The Rememberer knew about the door opening before any rider reached a garrison. Through the oral tradition — the memory of the pre-human world passed from Rememberer to Rememberer since before the Empire of the Sun existed. The orcs felt the door open the same day Root felt it through the working.

The warband is not fleeing the door. It is moving toward it. It was moving toward it before you were.

> *"You are the hunter. The one who renewed the Warden's binding."*
>
> *"I don't remember renewing it. But yes."*

The Rememberer's face shifts. Recognition of something that has been expected.

> *"We have been waiting for you. For eight hundred years we have been waiting for the right sequence of events and the right person to be in the middle of them."*

You tell them everything. The network as cage. The propagation. The door. The emerged beings. Root and the question. The agreement — beings will wait, Root maintains the working, the army must not advance.

The Rememberer listens. Places your account into the framework of eight hundred years of preserved knowledge. Then says the thing that stops the conversation.

> *"The Rememberers remember what the hunter forgot. We have kept it for you. In case you needed it returned."*

---

## Part Two: The Memory Returned

You ride south with the Rememberer and four warriors. Twenty warriors continue north to Ashford. Runners go ahead to tell the beings that the Rememberers are coming and the waiting has purpose.

That evening, beside a fire in a dry streambed, the Rememberer tells you what you gave away.

Not the facts — Lenne's account has the facts. What the Rememberer has is the interior. The quality of the pre-dawn dark outside Ashford and the calculation of a hunter deciding to go in anyway. The understanding in the canal that Lenne in the goldsmith's house was a person who needed someone to come and you were the person available. The moment in the Warden's tower when you shot the book instead of the figure.

The moment at the well.

The Rememberer slows here. The fire is low. Everyone around it is listening.

*A hunter standing at the edge of a well in a dark trading hall with the binding renewal on his tongue and the full awareness that the words would work — and the equally full awareness that the cost was everything he had become in the last five days.*

*He said the words.*

*Not because the cost was small. Because the cost was right.*

When it finishes the fire has burned to coals.

> *"You gave that away,"* Root says.
>
> *"Yes."*
>
> *"And you would do it again."*
>
> *"Yes."*

The Rememberer looks at you across the dead fire.

> *"The memory is yours now. Not returned — recounted. The difference matters and doesn't matter. You know what you did. You know what it cost. You know what you decided. The Rememberers will not carry it anymore. It belongs to you."*

---

## Part Three: The Lord Commander

Greyvane. Two days south of the watch post. The army encampment spread beyond the city walls — larger than when you rode north. The Lord Commander's standard flies two hours further south, at a coastal estate.

**Lord Commander Vael** is sixty, compact, with the specific quality of a man who has been in command long enough that command has become invisible. He is standing at a campaign table covered in maps when you enter.

> *"The hunter. Cross sent seven letters about you. Six in the last four days. The seventh arrived this morning."* He sets down his stylus. *"The army does not march in three days."*

Cross held it.

You tell him everything. The network as cage. The propagation. The door. The emerged beings. Root and the question. The agreement.

He listens the way he reads — completely, rapidly, missing nothing. When you finish he asks three questions.

What happens if the army encounters the beings. What is Root. Why did the orcs say nothing for eight hundred years.

> *"No one asked them,"* you say. *"And they knew humans wouldn't listen."*

The Lord Commander looks at the maps.

> *"Are you asking me to listen?"*
>
> *"I'm asking you to hold the army while we find the answer."*

He looks at you for a long moment.

> *"Bring them in. The elf and the orc. I'll hear them."*

The meeting takes two hours. Root speaks about the stasis working with the precision of someone who has maintained it for ten thousand years. The Rememberer speaks about the oral tradition with the authority of eight hundred years of preserved knowledge.

At the end, the Rememberer says:

> *"Every negotiation between humans and orcs in eight hundred years has been conducted with weapons either drawn or present. No one came to the table asking what the plains were. They came asking who the plains belonged to. The question of who was always going to be answered with blood. The question of what requires something else."*

> *"Something else,"* the Lord Commander says.
>
> *"A hunter with a survey commission and no particular authority and the specific habit of going toward things instead of away from them."*

> *"The army holds. Indefinitely, pending resolution."* He looks at each of you in turn. *"Go to Deepstone. Find the answer. Come back and tell me what I'm negotiating and with whom and on what terms."*

---

## Part Four: The Road to Deepstone

Three days west of Greyvane. The western mountains rising on the horizon.

On the second day, Edwick rides alongside you.

> *"The dwarfs. The enchanted stone. The alloys that stop the dead. The tunnel constructions that dampen magic. No failed early versions, no primitive precursors. The dwarfs emerged with the craft complete."*

He pauses.

> *"I think the network built the dwarfs to open the cage from the inside. When the time was right. When the surface had changed enough. When something like a way might be possible."*

The Deepstone trade post: stone buildings, forge smoke, the particular industrious density of dwarven construction.

**Clan-master Sera Deepstone** reads the Lord Commander's letter twice and leads you to the forge.

At the central station: a dwarf working enchanted stone into the precise geometry of a hollow point instrument. Made unconsciously, hands doing what they know how to do without the mind directing them.

> *"How long has your clan been making those?"*
>
> *"Four hundred years at this post. Longer in the mountain halls. We don't know what they measure."*

You tell her. The forge goes quiet. Eight dwarfs listen for two hours.

> *"The readings changed three months ago,"* Sera says. *"Every instrument in every hall across both mountain ranges."*

The renewal. The propagation beginning.

The clan records: six hundred years of readings. Three hundred years ago, a slow decline begins — consistent with the stasis working weakening. And an anomaly: X=200, Y=500. A localized elevation rising independently for fifty years.

Sera commits to riding to the elder-council in twelve days.

The stone-setter treats the knee. Forty minutes of work. The joint improves from Severe to Moderate. The permanent-failure risk retreats.

---

## Part Five: The Unworked

You ride west into the foothills. Two days. A path on the mountain slope — not an animal track. Too deep, too consistent, too purposeful. Something walked here on two feet. Repeatedly. Following the hollow point channel from above.

The path leads to a shelf of rock. A hollow carved into the face. Inside: a dwarven measuring instrument planted at a specific angle and a wall of Khazdul notation — dates and numbers, three different hands, fifty years of unauthorized records. Last entry: eight days ago.

**Kael** climbs to the shelf and finds you waiting.

Young for a dwarf. A scholar's braiding. Twelve years of field work they never reported.

> *"The readings have been climbing for fifty years. Three months ago they changed quality. The signature became intentional — as though whatever produces the resonance became aware of being measured."*
>
> *"Four days ago the readings changed again. The resonance is no longer just intentional. It is directional. It is pointing up."*

Kael's predecessor Vor trained them. Vor's predecessor Maret Deepstone found the channel fifty years ago and was afraid to report anomalous readings she couldn't explain. So she kept measuring. Trained her successor. The line continued.

Below the shelf: a cavern system. The deepest section unmapped. The clan calls it the Unworked.

Four markers arranged around the shaft's edge. Enchanted stone. Warm. Placed four days ago.

> *"These are keys,"* Kael says, holding one. *"I don't know how I know. But I know."*

---

## Part Six: The Chamber

Kael places the key in the shaft's edge. The sound of something fitting into the place it was made for. The shaft glows — warm light descending, revealing worked stone, ancient, precise. The glow reaches the bottom: a circular chamber, eight meters across, larger markers in geometric arrangement. One of them moving.

The descent: carved handholds, twenty meters. The knee manages. The chamber warm and ancient, walls covered in worn Khazdul notation.

At the center: a figure. Seated. Dwarven in form. Eyes open and luminescent.

It transmits.

*You came. We have been waiting.*

*We are what the network built to speak with what the network contains. The beings above the plains and the beings in the mountains — we are connected. The cage separated us.*

You tell it what has already happened. The Lord Commander. The Rememberer. Root. The elder-council. The question asked and the beings agreeing to wait.

The figure receives it.

*We did not know if the surface would be ready. The pieces were placed. But the pieces are living things and living things make choices that sequences cannot predict. You chose correctly. Many times.*

Then the answer.

*The beings below the plains are everywhere on the surface simultaneously. When they resume, they resume everywhere. There is no negotiation of specific territories because they are not territorial — they are ambient.*

*But the mountains are specific. The mountains were never theirs. And the mountains are large enough, if opened, to hold every people currently on the surface.*

*The network built the dwarfs to build this. The craft they've maintained — the enchanted stone, the deep halls, the tunnel engineering — is exactly what you'd give something if you needed it to create homes for every displaced people on the surface.*

*Is there room — yes. In the mountains. Inside the particular.*

> *"What are you?"* you ask. *"Specifically."*

*We are what the network was before it was a cage. We are the world's memory of itself.*

*The network is our body. The beings are our before. The dwarfs are our hands. The elves are our patience. The surface-dwellers are our hope.*

*Go. Tell them what the world needs them to know. We will be here if you need to return.*

---

## Epilogue: The Answer Exists

The answer:

The beings below the plains will return to the surface. This is not negotiable. But the mountains will not change — they were never the beings' territory. And the mountains are large enough, expanded over generations by dwarfs built for exactly this purpose, to hold every people currently living on the plains.

Not displacement. Migration. The specific places inside the particular mountains, built by the people who were made to build them, for the people who will need them.

The plains return to the beings. The surface-dwellers move inside. Room is found — not on the surface, but within it.

The sequence was designed:
- The cage to contain the beings
- The socket for the elves to provide outer suppression
- The dwarfs to build the specific places
- The Rememberers to preserve the memory of what the plains were
- The renewal to activate the network
- The propagation to wake the dwarfs to their purpose
- The door to ask the question
- The Go-Between to carry the answer to everyone who needed to hear it

A plan ten thousand years in the making.

The answer exists. Now it needs to reach everyone who can act on it.

*The elder-council meets in ten days.*

*End of Chapter Four*
