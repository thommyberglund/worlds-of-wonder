# Aldric — Chapter Six
## A Chronicle of the Central Plains

---

## Prologue

*The mountain halls settle around you as the days pass.*

*You stay three days after the vote — not because the council requires it but because the work that follows a vote is different from the work that precedes it, and you've learned to read which kind of work needs you present and which kind needs you out of the way.*

---

## Part One: After the Vote

The expansion survey authorization generates its own momentum. Sera's clan members mapping access points. The Irongate representatives sharing eastern range schematics. Kael producing fifty years of instrument readings in a format the official survey teams can use. Edwick writes. The mountain halls fill with the productive noise of a people given permission to do what their craft has been preparing them to do.

On the second day, **Dura Stoneback** — one of the two dissenting elders — finds you in the corridor.

> *"My objection stands. The tool question. I don't believe it was answered adequately. But I voted no and was overruled and the process was correct and I will not obstruct the survey."* A pause. *"I will, however, be watching. If the migration framework is not genuinely voluntary — if any people are pressured or displaced — I will use every authority available to me to halt it."*
>
> *"Good,"* you say.

She blinks.

> *"The framework needs someone watching for that. Someone with the authority to halt it and the willingness to use it. The two dissenters should be on the oversight body when the framework is drafted."*

She is quiet.

> *"You are trying to recruit me,"* she says.
>
> *"I'm trying to make the framework as solid as possible."*

> *"I'll consider it,"* she says. And walks away.

*Thirteen to two is better with both dissenters in the room than with them outside it.*

---

## Part Two: The Ride to Ashford

You ride east on the third day. Not back to Deepstone — directly east, back toward the plains.

The Rememberer's most recent report: goblins have left the palisade and positioned outside it. A single goblin sitting on the dead grass boundary. The other thirteen in an arc. All of them watching the Rememberer watching the beings.

Reading the reader.

You arrive at Ashford in the early afternoon.

Twenty-two beings now — five more since Lenne's count. Arranged in three distinct clusters, each oriented slightly differently. The Rememberer at the edge of the orc camp, watching.

And on the dead grass boundary line: one goblin. Small. Sitting cross-legged. Impossibly still for a goblin.

It turns and looks at you.

The channel opens — quick and precise, shaped differently from the beings' vast distributed transmission. The way a goblin moves: fast, contextual, heavily conditional.

*You took a long time,* it transmits. *We have been here since the door opened.*

You dismount. Walk to the dead grass edge. Sit down cross-legged six meters away.

The cold comes up through the ground immediately. The grass beneath you exactly at the transition — green on your left, dead grey on your right.

The goblin turns fully to face you and the transmission sharpens.

*Better. You understand the ground.*

---

## Part Three: Route

The goblin tells you what the goblins have been doing at Ashford for two weeks.

They have been in the cave systems beneath the plains for eight hundred years. The hollow points run through the caves. They call them the old voices — the places where something very old speaks through rock.

> *"We have been listening to the old voices for eight hundred years. The orcs feel the pressure from above and below. We feel the voices. Different things. Different knowledge."*

They came when the door opened — not because anyone summoned them, but because the old voices were no longer in the rock. They were here, on the surface, in bodies. Capable of being spoken to directly.

The goblin explains what they want to tell the beings.

> *"We want to tell them we remember. Eight hundred years of living in the caves above their cage. The old voices through stone. We remember what they were before the cage. The deep clans have kept the memory the way the orcs' Rememberers keep the memory — not in words, in the underground itself. In the specific routes our clans follow through the cave systems. In the places we avoid and the places we gather and the songs we sing in the dark that have words nobody translates because the words are older than translation."*

A pause.

> *"We want to tell them that we never forgot them. And we want to ask if they remember us."*

You try to carry the goblin's message through the channel to the beings.

You fail. The transmission disperses. What reaches the beings is not the message but the awareness that there is a message — and the attempt itself.

The beings bypass you entirely and speak directly to the goblin.

The goblin receives it.

*They remember. They remember the caves. They remember the songs in the dark.* A pause carrying eight hundred years. *They did not know we were still here. They thought the surface was empty.*

The beings ask: who else is here. They want to know who has been living on the surface. All of it.

---

## Part Four: The Full Account

You open the channel yourself and transmit the full inventory.

*Humans.* Short-lived, urgent, ambitious. The largest population on the surface. A faith called the Fate — collective belief made tangible. Most of them don't know the beings are here.

*Elves.* Not from this world originally. One of them is maintaining the stasis working now, alone, having left her grove for the first time in ten thousand years. She came south because the old approach stopped working and she chose to try something else.

*Dwarfs.* In the mountains above the hollow point network. The network built them. Their craft knows things their minds haven't caught up with. They voted thirteen to two to begin the expansion. They have been building future homes without knowing that's what they were doing.

*Orcs.* Original inhabitants alongside the goblins. Displaced twice — by the elves, then by the humans. They came back when the empire fell. They have Rememberers who preserved the memory of the beings for eight hundred years. They were walking toward the door before anyone asked them to.

*Goblins.* Original inhabitants alongside the orcs. In the cave systems for eight hundred years. Following routes that trace the hollow point channels. Singing songs in the dark that remember what the plains were. They came when the door opened.

*Vampires. Dragons. The First Emperors in their desert tombs.* Different problems. Different clocks. One thing at a time.

*This is what the surface holds. This is who has been living here. This is the world you are returning to.*

The beings receive it. Recognition, grief, curiosity — and a recalibration. Twenty-two presences that came through the door expecting to return to what they remembered, now understanding that what they remember is gone and what is here instead is not an absence but a presence.

*We did not know,* the nearest being transmits. *We hoped for someone to find a way. We did not know there would be this many someones.*

And then, to the goblins:

*The small ones who live in the caves — they know our songs.*

The small goblin begins to sing.

The beings go still. One by one the clusters converge. The distributed awareness contracts toward a single point.

The arc of fourteen goblins moves forward to the boundary.

Across the dead grass. Across ten thousand years. Looking at each other.

The beings transmit directly to the goblins. The fourteen goblins receive it.

And they sing back.

---

## Part Five: Route's Name

The goblin gives its name.

> *"In my language it doesn't translate. In the Common Tongue the closest is — Route. We name for function. What we are for. My function is this: I find the connections between things."*
>
> *"My function is this too,"* you transmit.
>
> *"Yes. I know."*

The Rememberer tells a story by the fire — the plains before the cage, in the Rememberers' oldest memory. The quality of the ground when it was paying attention.

The goblins stop singing to listen.

Route sings in response — the underground version. What the cold from below felt like before the cage. What the old voices sounded like from the cave systems. What it meant to hear the plains thinking.

The nearest being transmits to the night, to the fire, to all of them at the boundary:

*This is what we did not know. We knew we were heard. We did not know we were remembered.*

---

## Part Six: The Framework Gap

At dawn, Route transmits one more thing.

> *"The ones from the mountains — the ones who will build the homes. Do they know about the caves?"*

You explain the Deepstone records. The architectural surveys. The unconstructed levels.

> *"The caves connect. The cave systems beneath the plains connect to the cave systems beneath the mountains. We have traveled between them for eight hundred years. The homes the mountain-ones build — they will need to connect to the existing cave systems. Or they will be isolated."*

The migration framework as currently conceived by Edwick doesn't include this. The mountain hall expansion has been conceived as a self-contained dwarven project. But the people who will move inside the mountains need routes between the halls and to the surface — and the people who know those routes are sitting around a fire on the plains of Ashford.

> *"You know the routes,"* you transmit.
>
> *"We know all the routes. Every clan that runs deep has mapped them. We don't share the maps — they are clan property, survival knowledge. But the maps exist."*

How many deep clans: forty-three. Across both mountain ranges and the plains between them.

> *"Will you share them?"*
>
> *"That is a clan decision. Not mine alone."* A pause. *"I can speak for my clan. Not the others."*

The Deeproute clan. Western range channel, four hundred years. Route will speak for them.

You ride for the mountain halls.

---

## Part Seven: The Propagation Stabilizes

En route, you detour east through the survey sites.

Two weeks without current data. The hollow point reader Kael gave you at the shelf — one of the measuring stones — reads six sites across the afternoon.

Every site is holding at its last recorded reading. The propagation has stopped accelerating.

Not reversed. Not declining. Stable.

The network activated, the door opened, the sequence completed its critical phase — and then the propagation leveled off. The system achieved its purpose and stopped pushing.

You write to Ost and Cross and the survey teams: the readings have stabilized. The emergency phase is over. Transition from crisis monitoring to baseline documentation.

Then you ride west toward Edwick.

---

## Part Eight: The Framework Rebuilt

The mountain halls. Edwick, Kael, and **Brek** — the Irongate senior council member — are looking at a composite map on the floor. The unconstructed levels from three sources, trying to integrate what they have.

Edwick stopped the underground access section. He has been thinking for two days.

> *"If the deep clan cave systems connect the mountain ranges to the plains, then every person who moves inside needs routes that aren't exclusively dwarven. We have no data on the natural cave systems whatsoever."*

You lay it out. Route's request: Kael comes to Ashford. The Deeproute maps and the hall surveys assessed together before the framework is built wrong.

Brek: the eastern range equivalent is the **Stonepath clan**. Four hundred years of trading relationship with Irongate. They might listen if the request comes from dwarfs rather than from outside.

The reorganization:

- Edwick coordinates framework drafting, rewrites underground access section
- Kael goes to Ashford to meet Route, assess what the Deeproute maps cover
- Brek approaches Stonepath through the existing trade relationship
- You return to Ashford and tell the beings the framework is being rebuilt to include the routes

Sera from the doorway:

> *"The clan-master approves. We're all clear on our roles. There's food. Eat something before you go back out."*

---

## Part Nine: The Year Ahead

That evening, Edwick reads the framework outline by the fire.

The migration as voluntary and generational. The expansion survey as the first practical step. The oversight body — Dura Stoneback and her co-dissenter included. The timeline: generations, measured in lifespans. The underground access section: marked *pending goblin route maps, Deeproute clan, 43 clans total.*

> *"The humans,"* you say. *"When the expansion survey is complete and the framework is ready to present — will the human governing bodies still feel the urgency?"*

Edwick is honest: probably not. The immediate crisis is over. The army is held. The coastal kingdoms will move slowly without pressure.

> *"We find the people inside those institutions who understand. The same way Cross understood. The same way Vael understood. There are people in every institution capable of holding the long view if someone makes the case clearly enough. That's what the year is for — not just drafting the framework. Building the coalition that will receive it."*

Edwick looks at you.

> *"You're going to spend the next year talking to people. Lords, merchant councils, priests of the Fate."*
>
> *"Yes. And Lenne, who has her father's ear and understands the framework better than anyone except you. And the Rememberer, who can speak to the orc warbands. And Route, if the Deeproute clan agrees to participate. And you, who can explain the architecture of a ten-thousand-year sequence to anyone willing to listen."*

He is quiet.

> *"That's not nothing,"* he says.
>
> *"No,"* you say. *"It's not nothing."*

The fire burns. Outside, the mountains hold their ancient weight against the winter sky. Below them, the caves run their dark routes between the surface and the deep. And on the plains east of Ashford, at the boundary between living and dead grass, twenty-two pale figures and fourteen goblins and twenty orc warriors are in the slow continuous process of introducing themselves to each other.

The longer work.

You ride back to Ashford in the morning.

*End of Chapter Six*
