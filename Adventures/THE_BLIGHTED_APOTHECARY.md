
# The Blighted Apothecary: A Dark Fantasy Adventure
*Expanded Edition*

---

## Overview
**Scale:** Small (Personal Stakes)
**Location:** Thistle Hold, a coastal town on the edge of civilization
**Threat:** Magister Veyth, a necromancer posing as an antique dealer, and his blighted minions
**Tone:** Intimate, claustrophobic, and visceral. Every face has a name. Stakes are personal, not political.

---

## Background
The coastal town of **Thistle Hold** is no stranger to whispers of the dark. Lately, rumors have spread of a "blight beast" stalking the streets at night, leaving behind twisted, half-rotted corpses. The townsfolk blame the nearby ruins, but the truth is far closer to home. **Magister Veyth**, a seemingly harmless antique dealer, has been using his shop—the **Abandoned Apothecary**—as a front for necromantic experiments. His goal: to awaken the **Skull of the First Lich**, a relic hidden beneath the town.

The player characters are drawn into the mystery when they arrive by ship and are mistaken for mercenaries hired by **High Priestess Seraphine**, leader of the **Order of the Dawn**. As they uncover Veyth’s plot, they must navigate a web of deceit, horror, and moral dilemmas before the blight consumes Thistle Hold.

## Key NPCs

### High Priestess Seraphine
- **Role:** Leader of the Order of the Dawn in Thistle Hold
- **Motivation:** To purge the town of necromancy and protect the innocent
- **Skills:** Faith (Advanced) +4, Persuasion +3, Blade +2
- **Attributes:** Might 13, Finesse 12, Resolve 15, Cunning 11
- **Divine Mark:** Mark of the Judge (+1 to Might or Finesse rolls vs. the guilty; must punish wrongdoers)
- **Personality:** Seraphine is a woman of action, pragmatic and fiercely protective of her town. She is willing to bend the rules if it means stopping Veyth, but she draws the line at outright corruption.

### Magister Veyth
- **Role:** Necromancer and antique dealer
- **Motivation:** To complete the ritual and bind the Skull of the First Lich to his will
- **Skills:** Arcana (Advanced) +4, Deception +3, Intimidation +2
- **Attributes:** Might 11, Finesse 12, Resolve 15, Cunning 14
- **Special Abilities:**
  - **Blood Pact:** Can summon 1d4 blighted minions as a Moderate spell (DC 15, Cunning + Arcana)
  - **Necrotic Touch:** On a successful melee attack (Might +2 vs. Finesse), deals 1d6 damage and inflicts a Minor torso injury
- **Corruption:** Hollow Eyes (+1 Intimidation, -1 to Cunning-based social rolls)

### Lady Elara
- **Role:** Rogue scholar of the Arcane College, desperate to stop Veyth
- **Motivation:** She discovered Veyth’s experiments and tried to stop him, only to be poisoned by his blight
- **Skills:** Lore (Advanced) +4, Stealth +2
- **Attributes:** Might 10, Finesse 14, Resolve 13, Cunning 12
- **Status:** Gravely wounded (Severe torso injury; untreated, this will worsen to Critical on any failed roll)

### Gorim Ironfist
- **Role:** Dwarven smith whose apprentice was taken by Veyth
- **Motivation:** To find his apprentice, Tobin, and see justice done
- **Skills:** Craft (Advanced) +4, Might +3
- **Attributes:** Might 15, Finesse 10, Resolve 14, Cunning 11
- **Personality:** Gruff but kind, Gorim is desperate to save his apprentice and will offer weapons, armor, or coin to anyone who helps him.

### Harlan the Drunk
- **Role:** Town drunk with a knack for stumbling into trouble
- **Motivation:** To drown his sorrows—and his memories of what he’s seen
- **Skills:** Survival +1, Gossip +2
- **Attributes:** Might 9, Finesse 8, Resolve 10, Cunning 12
- **Personality:** Harlan is half-mad, but his ramblings often contain kernels of truth. He’s seen more than he lets on and is terrified of Veyth.

## Key Locations

### The Docks
- **Description:** The party arrives by ship at the bustling docks of Thistle Hold. The air is thick with the scent of salt, fish, and something darker—rot, perhaps, or the metallic tang of old blood.
- **Features:**
  - **Town Guards:** Mistake the party for mercenaries and escort them to the Temple of the Dawn.
  - **Locals:** Whisper about the blight beast and missing folk. A successful DC 10 Social check reveals rumors of strange lights in the Abandoned Apothecary.

### Temple of the Dawn
- **Description:** A squat stone building with stained-glass windows depicting saints and martyrs. Seraphine awaits the party here, mistaking them for hired mercenaries.
- **Features:**
  - **Seraphine’s Office:** Contains a map of Thistle Hold, marked with the location of the Abandoned Apothecary.
  - **Order Archives:** Records of recent disappearances and reports of blighted corpses.

### The Abandoned Apothecary
- **Description:** A dilapidated building on the outskirts of town, its windows boarded and its door hanging off its hinges. The air reeks of decay and old herbs.
- **Features:**
  - **Main Room:** Cluttered with alchemical equipment, dusty jars, and yellowed books. A trapdoor in the corner leads to the cellar.
  - **Cellar:** A makeshift laboratory filled with ritual circles and the stench of death. Cages line the walls, some empty, some containing half-rotted corpses.

### The Ruins Beneath
- **Description:** A network of ancient catacombs, long forgotten by the townsfolk. The walls are lined with the bones of Veyth’s failed experiments.
- **Features:**
  - **Ritual Chamber:** The heart of Veyth’s operations. The Skull of the First Lich sits on a stone altar, surrounded by lit candles and arcane sigils.
  - **Blighted Minions:** 1d4 blighted creatures patrol the ruins, drawn to the skull’s power.

## Plot Hooks

### A Militant’s Request
The party arrives in Thistle Hold by ship and is mistaken for mercenaries hired by High Priestess Seraphine. She offers them silver to investigate the Abandoned Apothecary and stop Magister Veyth before he completes his ritual. If the party refuses or reveals they’re not the mercenaries she expected, Seraphine warns them to leave town before nightfall—or help her anyway.

### A Smith’s Desperation
Gorim Ironfist, a dwarven smith, seeks the party’s help. His apprentice, Tobin, was taken by the blight beast, and he believes Veyth is responsible. Gorim offers weapons, armor, or coin in exchange for their aid.

### The Drunk’s Ramblings
Harlan, the town drunk, babbles about "the hollow-eyed man" and a "skull that whispers." His ravings hint at the truth behind Veyth’s experiments.

## Scenes

### Scene 1: Arrival and Mistaken Identity
- **Description:** The party disembarks at the docks and is met by town guards, who mistake them for mercenaries and escort them to the Temple of the Dawn.
- **Threat Level:** Easy (DC 10)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Play Along:** Accept Seraphine’s offer and agree to investigate the apothecary.
  2. **Refuse:** Risk Seraphine’s wrath or attempt to convince her of their innocence (DC 14 Persuasion).
  3. **Gather Information:** Question the guards or locals about the blight beast and Veyth’s activities (DC 10 Social).
- **XP Reward:** 5–10 XP

### Scene 2: The Temple of the Dawn
- **Description:** Seraphine briefs the party on Veyth’s activities and offers them silver to stop him. She provides a map of Thistle Hold and warns them to be discreet.
- **Threat Level:** Easy (DC 10)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Accept the Job:** Agree to investigate the apothecary and stop Veyth.
  2. **Negotiate:** Attempt to secure better terms or additional resources (DC 12 Persuasion).
  3. **Seek Allies:** Ask Seraphine about Gorim Ironfist or other potential allies in town.
- **XP Reward:** 5–10 XP

### Scene 3: The Abandoned Apothecary
- **Description:** The apothecary looms before the party, its boarded windows and rotting door a stark contrast to the bustling town around it. The air is thick with the scent of decay.
- **Threat Level:** Moderate (DC 15)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Sneak In:** DC 14 Stealth. Success: Enter unnoticed. Failure: Alert 1d4 blighted minions.
  2. **Break In:** DC 12 Might. Success: Force open the door, but the noise attracts 1d4 blighted minions.
  3. **Pose as Buyers:** DC 14 Deception. Success: Gain entry and a chance to explore the main room. Failure: Veyth grows suspicious and prepares an ambush.
- **XP Reward:** 25–40 XP

### Scene 4: The Cellar Laboratory
- **Description:** The cellar is a nightmare of alchemical equipment and ritual circles. Veyth stands with his back to the entrance, deep in the early stages of the ritual.
- **Threat Level:** Moderate (DC 15)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Confront Veyth:** Announce your presence and force a confrontation. Veyth calls his minions, and combat ensues.
  2. **Disrupt the Ritual:** DC 15 Cunning (Arcana). Success: The ritual collapses, and Veyth is stunned for one round. Failure: Veyth completes the first stage of the ritual, summoning 1d4 blighted minions.
  3. **Search for Clues:** DC 12 Cunning. Success: Find Veyth’s journal, detailing his experiments and the location of the Skull of the First Lich.
- **XP Reward:** 25–40 XP

### Scene 5: The Ritual Chamber
- **Description:** The heart of the ruins, where Veyth stands before the altar, chanting in a guttural tongue. The Skull of the First Lich glows with an eerie light.
- **Threat Level:** Hard (DC 20)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Battle Veyth:** Combat against Veyth and his minions. Veyth uses Blood Pact and Necrotic Touch to weaken the party.
  2. **Negotiate with Veyth:** DC 16 Persuasion or Intimidation. Success: Veyth offers a deal—spare his life in exchange for the skull. Failure: Veyth attacks, enraged by the party’s interference.
  3. **Seize the Skull:** DC 18 Finesse. Success: Grab the skull, but its dark energy inflicts a Moderate torso injury. Failure: The skull’s power throws the character back, dealing a Severe torso injury.
- **XP Reward:** 75–100 XP

## Aftermath

### Destroy the Skull
The blighted minions collapse, and Thistle Hold is saved. Seraphine rewards the party and offers them a place in the Order of the Dawn. However, the Order now watches them closely, wary of their exposure to necromancy. Gorim’s apprentice, Tobin, is found alive but marked by his ordeal. Gorim pays the party a modest reward and does not ask questions.

### Keep the Skull
The party gains a powerful artifact, but its corruption begins to take hold. Veyth’s spirit lingers, whispering dark promises in their dreams. Gorim’s apprentice is never found, and Gorim leaves Thistle Hold within the week.

### Flee the Town
The party escapes with their lives, but the blight spreads unchecked. Thistle Hold falls into chaos, and the party is branded as outlaws. Gorim’s apprentice is among the first new victims of the blight.

## GM Notes
- **Magic:** All magic follows the rules in MAGIC.md. Arcane magic is dangerous and corrupting, while faith magic requires devotion and resolve.
- **Injuries:** Use the injury system from RULES.md. Permanent injuries and corruption are likely.
- **Morality:** The adventure presents difficult choices. Destroying the skull is the "right" thing to do, but keeping it offers power at a terrible cost.
- **Tone:** Keep the narrative visceral and cynical. Describe the stench of rot, the bite of the wind, and the metallic taste of blood. Heroes are rare in this world—most end up dying in the mud.
