
# The Blighted Apothecary: A Small Adventure for Dark Fantasy

## Overview
**Scale:** Small (Personal Stakes)
**Location:** The Abandoned Apothecary, Thistle Hold
**Threat:** Magister Veyth, a necromancer posing as an antique dealer, and his blighted minions.
**Tone:** Intimate, claustrophobic, and visceral. Every face has a name. Stakes are personal, not political.

---

## Background
The coastal town of Thistle Hold is no stranger to whispers of the dark. Lately, rumors have spread of a "blight beast" stalking the streets at night, leaving behind twisted, half-rotted corpses. The townsfolk blame the nearby ruins, but the truth is far closer to home. Magister Veyth, a seemingly harmless antique dealer, has been using his shop—the Abandoned Apothecary—as a front for necromantic experiments. His goal: to awaken the Skull of the First Lich, a relic hidden beneath the town.

The player characters are drawn into the mystery when a dying scholar, **Lady Elara**, stumbles into their path, clutching a bloodstained map of the town’s underbelly. She gasps a warning about Veyth’s plot before collapsing, her last words a plea for help.

---

## Key NPCs

### Lady Elara
- **Role:** Rogue scholar of the Arcane College, desperate to stop Veyth.
- **Motivation:** She discovered Veyth’s experiments and tried to stop him, only to be poisoned by his blight.
- **Skills:** Knowledge (Arcana) +4, Stealth +2
- **Attributes:** Might 10, Finesse 14, Resolve 13, Cunning 12
- **Status:** Dying (Moderate torso injury; needs immediate stabilization or will perish within the hour).

### Magister Veyth
- **Role:** Necromancer and leader of the cult.
- **Motivation:** To complete the ritual and bind the Skull of the First Lich to his will.
- **Skills:** Arcana (Advanced) +4, Deception +3, Intimidation +2
- **Attributes:** Might 11, Finesse 12, Resolve 15, Cunning 14
- **Special Abilities:**
  - **Blood Pact:** Can summon 1d4 blighted minions (use "Blighted" stat block) as a Moderate spell (DC 15, Cunning + Arcana).
  - **Necrotic Touch:** On a successful melee attack (Might +2 vs. Finesse), deals 1d6 damage and inflicts a Minor torso injury (no roll required).
- **Corruption:** Hollow Eyes (+1 Intimidation, -1 to Cunning-based social rolls).

### High Priestess Seraphine
- **Role:** Leader of the Order of the Dawn in Thistle Hold.
- **Motivation:** To purge the town of necromancy and protect the innocent.
- **Skills:** Faith (Advanced) +4, Persuasion +3, Blade +2
- **Attributes:** Might 13, Finesse 12, Resolve 15, Cunning 11
- **Divine Mark:** Mark of the Judge (+1 to Might or Finesse rolls vs. the guilty; must punish wrongdoers).

## Key Locations

### The Abandoned Apothecary
- **Description:** A dilapidated building on the outskirts of Thistle Hold, its windows boarded and its door hanging off its hinges. The air reeks of decay and old herbs. Inside, shelves of dusty jars and bottles line the walls, but the true horrors lie beneath.
- **Features:**
  - **Main Room:** Cluttered with alchemical equipment and half-rotted specimens. A trapdoor in the corner leads to the cellar.
  - **Cellar:** A makeshift laboratory, filled with ritual circles and the stench of death. The Skull of the First Lich rests on a pedestal at the center, pulsing with dark energy.

### The Ruins Beneath
- **Description:** A network of ancient catacombs, long forgotten by the townsfolk. The walls are lined with the bones of Veyth’s failed experiments.
- **Features:**
  - **Ritual Chamber:** The heart of Veyth’s operations. The Skull of the First Lich sits on a stone altar, surrounded by lit candles and arcane sigils.
  - **Blighted Minions:** 1d4 blighted creatures (use "Blighted" stat block) patrol the ruins, drawn to the skull’s power.

## Stat Blocks

### Blighted Minion
- **Attributes:** Might 12, Finesse 8, Resolve 10, Cunning 6
- **Skills:** Stealth +1, Survival +2
- **Special Abilities:**
  - **Blighted Claws:** Melee attack (Might +2 vs. Finesse), deals 1d4 damage and inflicts a Minor injury (no roll required) on a hit.
  - **Unnatural Resilience:** Ignores the first Moderate injury it receives each encounter.
- **Weakness:** Fire (takes +1 damage from fire-based attacks).

## Plot Hooks
1. **A Dying Plea:** Lady Elara stumbles into the characters’ path, clutching a map and gasping for air. She begs them to stop Veyth before he completes the ritual. If they stabilize her (DC 15 Resolve), she provides crucial information about the apothecary and Veyth’s plans.
2. **A Militant’s Request:** High Priestess Seraphine approaches the characters, offering a reward for Veyth’s capture or death. She warns them of the dangers of necromancy and the blight beast.
3. **A Smith’s Desperation:** A local dwarven smith, **Gorim Ironfist**, seeks the characters’ help. His apprentice was taken by the blight beast, and he believes Veyth is responsible.

## Scenes

### Scene 1: The Dying Scholar
- **Description:** The characters find Lady Elara collapsed in an alley, her breath shallow and her skin pale. She clutches a bloodstained map of Thistle Hold, with the Abandoned Apothecary circled in red.
- **Threat Level:** Easy (DC 10)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Stabilize Lady Elara:** DC 15 Resolve. Success: She survives and shares her knowledge. Failure: She dies, but the map remains.
  2. **Search the Alley:** DC 12 Cunning. Success: Find a torn page from Veyth’s journal, detailing his experiments and the location of the apothecary.
  3. **Question the Locals:** DC 10 Social (Persuasion or Intimidation). Success: Learn rumors of strange lights and sounds coming from the apothecary at night.
- **XP Reward:** 5–10 XP (Easy encounter).

### Scene 2: The Abandoned Apothecary
- **Description:** The apothecary looms before the characters, its boarded windows and rotting door a stark contrast to the bustling town around it. The air is thick with the scent of decay.
- **Threat Level:** Moderate (DC 15)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Sneak In:** DC 14 Stealth. Success: Enter unnoticed. Failure: Alert 1d4 blighted minions.
  2. **Break In:** DC 12 Might. Success: Force open the door, but the noise attracts 1d4 blighted minions.
  3. **Investigate the Main Room:** DC 12 Cunning. Success: Find the trapdoor to the cellar and clues about Veyth’s experiments (e.g., journals, half-rotted corpses).
- **XP Reward:** 25–40 XP (Moderate encounter).

### Scene 3: The Cellar Laboratory
- **Description:** The cellar is a nightmare of alchemical equipment and ritual circles. The Skull of the First Lich pulses with dark energy, and the air hums with necromantic power.
- **Threat Level:** Hard (DC 20)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Confront Veyth:** Veyth is in the midst of a ritual, surrounded by blighted minions. Combat ensues.
  2. **Disrupt the Ritual:** DC 18 Cunning (Arcana). Success: The ritual fails, and the skull’s power wanes. Failure: Veyth completes the ritual, summoning additional blighted minions.
  3. **Destroy the Skull:** DC 20 Might (e.g., smashing it with a hammer). Success: The skull shatters, and the blighted minions collapse. Failure: The skull lashes out, dealing 1d6 damage to the character and inflicting a Minor torso injury.
- **XP Reward:** 75–100 XP (Hard encounter).

### Scene 4: The Ritual Chamber
- **Description:** The heart of the ruins, where Veyth stands before the altar, chanting in a guttural tongue. The Skull of the First Lich glows with an eerie light, and blighted minions patrol the chamber.
- **Threat Level:** Impossible (DC 25+)
- **Possible Actions:**
  1. **Battle Veyth:** Combat against Veyth and his minions. Veyth uses Blood Pact to summon reinforcements and Necrotic Touch to weaken the characters.
  2. **Negotiate with Veyth:** DC 20 Social (Persuasion or Intimidation). Success: Veyth offers a deal—spare his life in exchange for the skull. Failure: Veyth attacks, enraged by the characters’ interference.
  3. **Seize the Skull:** DC 22 Finesse. Success: The character grabs the skull, but its dark energy inflicts a Moderate torso injury. Failure: The skull lashes out, dealing 2d6 damage and inflicting a Severe torso injury.
- **XP Reward:** 250+ XP (Impossible encounter).

---

## Aftermath
- **Destroy the Skull:** The blighted minions collapse, and the town is saved. High Priestess Seraphine rewards the characters and offers them a place in the Order of the Dawn. However, the Order now watches them closely, wary of their exposure to necromancy.
- **Keep the Skull:** The characters gain a powerful artifact, but its corruption begins to take hold. Veyth’s spirit lingers, whispering dark promises in their dreams.
- **Flee the Town:** The characters escape with their lives, but the blight spreads unchecked. Thistle Hold falls into chaos, and the characters are branded as outlaws.

---

## Consequences
- **Personal:** Characters may gain permanent injuries, corruption, or divine marks.
- **Town:** Thistle Hold is forever changed. If the skull is destroyed, the town begins to heal. If not, the blight spreads, and the characters become hunted.
- **Factions:** The Order of the Dawn’s influence grows, or the Iron Pact seizes control, depending on the characters’ choices.

## GM Notes
- **Magic:** All magic follows the rules in MAGIC.md. Arcane magic is dangerous and corrupting, while faith magic requires devotion and resolve.
- **Injuries:** Use the injury system from RULES.md. Permanent injuries and corruption are likely.
- **Morality:** The adventure presents difficult choices. Destroying the skull is the "right" thing to do, but keeping it offers power at a terrible cost.
- **Tone:** Keep the narrative visceral and cynical. Describe the stench of rot, the bite of the wind, and the metallic taste of blood. Heroes are rare in this world—most end up dying in the mud.
