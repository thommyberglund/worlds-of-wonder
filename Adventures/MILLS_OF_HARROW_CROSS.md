# THE MILLS OF HARROW CROSS
### Chronicle of the Central Plains — A Small Adventure for 1–4 Players

*The wind comes off the plains carrying two things: the smell of turned earth from the southern fields, and something else — older, sweeter, wrong. The colonists at Harrow Cross stopped answering the mill road three days ago. The supply wagon from Thornwall is late. And in the granary at the crossroads, something moves at night that does not move like anything alive.*

**Scale:** Small | **Setting:** Frontier plains, X=510, Y=770 | **Sessions:** 1–2

---

## INTRODUCTION

The Mills of Harrow Cross is a self-contained adventure set in the recolonisation zone of the Central Plains, approximately at coordinates X=510, Y=770 — a day's ride north of Thornwall along the mill road. It is designed for one to four player characters of level one to three.

This is a small adventure. The world does not depend on the outcome. Whether the characters succeed or fail, Thornwall will still stand, the armies will still muster, and the dead will still walk somewhere out on the plains. What changes is what happens to the thirty-odd people sheltering in the mill complex at Harrow Cross, and whether any of them get home.

The adventure follows the Abercrombie–Sapkowski tradition: moral ambiguity, brutal consequence, and the grinding reality of survival in a world that does not care about heroism. The GM should play the world honestly. If the characters make mistakes, the world should punish them for it.

### Adventure Background

Harrow Cross is a crossroads village at Y=770 — or was, before the undead came north from the deeper plains eight months ago. The adult population fled south to Thornwall. But not everyone made it.

Emric Voss, a miller's apprentice of nineteen, stayed behind to protect the mill complex — a cluster of three buildings around the old stone cross that gives the village its name. With him are his younger sister Nella (twelve), an elderly priest of the Fate named Brother Aldous whose legs gave out during the evacuation, and five others: two farmhands, a carpenter, and a young married couple who refused to leave their stored grain.

They have been sheltering in the mill's ground floor for eleven days. The mill still turns — the stream has not stopped — and there is food enough for weeks. What there is not enough of is light. Their oil is almost gone.

But oil is not the only problem. Three nights ago, something entered the granary next door. They heard it moving. They heard it making sounds that were almost words. In the morning, one of the farmhands — Perrick — was gone. His boots remained by the door. His bedroll was cold and had never been slept in. He had simply not come back from the outhouse.

The thing in the granary is a vampire. Recently turned — perhaps two months old, no more. It was a colonist from a village further south, turned during the undead surge and left to wander. It followed the smell of living blood to Harrow Cross and has been trapped in the granary because it is, as yet, too feral and too newly made to think clearly about exits. It killed Perrick because he was outside alone after dark. It has not attacked the mill itself because something about the combined presence of Brother Aldous's weak but continuous faith working gives it discomfort.

The characters arrive because they were sent, hired, or drawn here by one of the hooks below. They arrive at dusk on the day this adventure begins.

### Adventure Hooks

Choose one, or let the characters' backgrounds determine which applies:

- **HIRED:** A Thornwall merchant named Goss is owed money by the carpenter at Harrow Cross and has hired the characters to collect the debt — or determine why the carpenter has not returned to the city. Payment: 15 silver marks each on safe return of either the debt or proof of the carpenter's death.

- **CONTRACTED:** Captain Undh's garrison at Thornwall needs confirmation of the situation at Harrow Cross before sending a relief column. The characters carry a letter of inquiry and are promised 20 silver marks each plus garrison endorsement for whatever they discover.

- **PERSONAL:** One of the survivors at Harrow Cross is known to a character — a sibling, a former colleague, someone owed a favour. No payment, just the weight of knowing they are there.

- **CONVENIENCE:** The characters are already travelling the mill road north. They see the mill lantern from the road and approach because a light in an allegedly abandoned village is worth investigating.

## THE SETTING

Harrow Cross sits at a genuine crossroads: the mill road running north-south, and an older track that runs east into the low hills and west toward the river. The village proper is seven buildings. The mill complex — the relevant location for this adventure — occupies the northwest corner of the crossroads.

### Area Overview

The GM should present Harrow Cross at dusk. The light is failing fast over the flat plains. The village is quiet in the wrong way — not the quiet of sleep, but the quiet of abandonment. Doors stand open. A dog kennel in front of the nearest house is empty and the chain has been gnawed through. The old stone cross at the centre of the crossroads lists to one side, and someone has scratched a new symbol into it — a circle with a line through it. Tracker or Lore characters will recognise this as a traveller's warning mark: the dead have been here.

The mill lantern is lit. That is the only sign of life.

---

### Location 1: The Mill (Primary Refuge)

A solid stone-and-timber building two storeys tall, with the waterwheel turning steadily at its eastern wall despite the hour. The ground floor is the working mill — grindstone, flour bins, tool racks. The upper floor is a sleeping loft with straw pallets and a ladder that can be pulled up. The heavy outer door is barred from within.

The survivors are here. When the characters knock or call out, there will be a long pause — nearly two minutes — before Emric Voss opens a shutter above the door and looks down at them. He is gaunt, hollow-eyed, and holding a woodsman's axe he does not know how to use. He will not open the door until he has asked the characters at least three questions and received answers that do not contradict each other.

> *Read aloud when the survivors finally let the characters in:*
>
> The bar lifts. The door opens. The smell is immediate — unwashed bodies, old straw, the sour ghost of burned tallow. Seven people in a space meant for working, not living. A priest in the corner, eyes closed, lips moving. A girl asleep under a grain sack. Two men by the wall who look at your weapons before they look at your faces.
>
> The boy who let you in — he cannot be more than twenty — puts his back against the closed door and pulls the bar down again before he speaks. His knuckles are white on the axe handle.
>
> *"You came from the south,"* he says. It is not a question. *"Did you see anything on the road? After dark, I mean. Did you see anything following you?"*

**Survivors (summary):**

- **Emric Voss** — de facto leader. Will cooperate with the characters but is watching them closely. Not stupid.
- **Brother Aldous** — priest of the Fate, Advanced Faith. Old, partially immobile, but the most useful person in the building.
- **Nella Voss** — Emric's sister. Quiet, observant. Has been watching the granary from the upper window and knows more about the thing's movements than anyone else.
- **Remaining survivors** — frightened, exhausted, and unlikely to take independent action unless directly pressured.

---

### Location 2: The Granary

A timber-framed building with a stone base, separated from the mill by a gap of three metres. The main door is on the south face. A small ventilation hatch sits near the roofline on the north face. The granary still holds roughly half a season's grain.

The door has been broken from inside. Something strong pushed through the latch bolt, bending the iron. The door now hangs slightly open and sways in the wind, which makes a sound at night that the survivors have come to dread.

The vampire is here during daylight. It has burrowed into the grain at the north end of the building, covering itself to block any light. At night it emerges and moves through the village, unable to leave the area entirely because it is disoriented and the faint pressure of Brother Aldous's faith works on it like a persistent headache — not painful enough to drive it away, but wrong enough that it circles rather than advancing.

> *Read aloud — the granary in daylight:*
>
> The grain dust catches the afternoon light coming through the ventilation slats in long pale shafts. The floor is covered in loose grain and the marks of something that has moved through it repeatedly — a trail from the door to the north end and back, over and over, like an animal pacing a cage.
>
> At the north wall, the grain is piled higher than it should be. Something has disturbed it into a rough mound. You can see, just barely, a shape beneath the surface. The grain moves with a very slight, very slow rhythm. Whatever is under there is not breathing. But something is moving.

The vampire in daylight (beneath the grain) is in a torpid half-state. It is not fully alert. Characters who approach quietly (Stealth DC 12) can examine the mound before it wakes. Characters who make significant noise or enter quickly will find it stirring within a round.

---

### Location 3: The Stone Cross

The old religious marker at the centre of the crossroads. It predates the current colonisation wave — possibly by four hundred years, though its original faith is unclear. The stone is dark granite, not local material, and the carving is worn to near-illegibility.

Brother Aldous, if asked about it, says the cross was here when the first wave of colonists came north eight hundred years ago, and there are records in Thornwall's scholars' quarter suggesting it was already ancient then. He does not know what faith it served. He says it does not interfere with the Fate's workings. He says this carefully, without making eye contact, which suggests he is not entirely certain.

The cross has a hollow at its base. A **Lore DC 14** roll identifies the carving inside as a First Empire warding mark — a seal intended to suppress the dead within a defined radius. It has not worked in centuries, its power long faded. But it explains why a vampire is circling rather than attacking: the cross is dead weight now, but the shape of what it was is still there, like an old scar in the world's memory.

## THE ANTAGONIST

### Hett — The Granary Warden

The vampire was a woman named Hett. She was twenty-six years old when she was turned, a fence-mender's wife from a village called Millbrook, three leagues south of Harrow Cross. Millbrook does not exist anymore. Whatever happened there, she does not remember it clearly. What she remembers is fragments: her husband's name (Cale), the smell of bread, a night that went very dark and very wrong, and hunger.

She has been a vampire for approximately sixty days. In that time she has killed twice that she is certain of. She is not a monster in the philosophical sense — she is a human being in the process of becoming something else, caught in the transformation before full self-awareness has returned, operating mostly on instinct and the terrible pressure of the blood-need.

The GM should resist the temptation to make Hett simply a combat encounter. She is dangerous — genuinely, lethally dangerous to low-level characters who engage her without preparation. But she is not evil in the human sense. She is suffering. Whether the characters have the resources (and the will) to address that is one of the adventure's central questions.

#### Stat Block

| Attribute | Score | Modifier |
|---|---|---|
| Might | 16 | +3 |
| Finesse | 14 | +2 |
| Resolve | 8 | −1 |
| Cunning | 10 | +0 |

**Type:** Vampire (recently turned)
**Threat:** Hard (DC 20) in direct combat
**Skills:** Unarmed Combat +4 (vestigial Blade +2)

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Sunlight: Severe torso injury per round of exposure
- Fire / decapitation / heart destruction: Effective — normal injury rules apply
- Active holy symbol (faith user present): −2 to all attack rolls while within range
- Feeding: Required every 3 days; without it she begins to diminish toward torpor

**Special:** Hett cannot be persuaded through normal social interaction — her Cunning and Resolve are too low for rational discourse. She can, however, be reached through an unexpected stimulus: her husband's name spoken aloud causes her to freeze for one round. A character who knows this and uses it carefully may hold a brief, fragmented conversation with the human part of her that remains.

---

### Hett's Behaviour

**During the day** she is torpid in the granary. She will not attack unless the characters enter and make deliberate contact with her — even then, her first response is to press further into the grain rather than fight.

**At night** she emerges and moves through the village. She is drawn toward the mill by the smell of blood. The faith-pressure from Brother Aldous keeps her from closing within fifteen metres of the mill door. She circles. She waits. If a character is outside after dark without precautions, she will approach.

If directly confronted at night — outside, in the open — she will fight. She fights with her hands and her teeth, with the full strength of the vampiric transformation. She does not have the cunning of an old vampire. She does not plan. She closes distance and uses her Might advantage.

---

### Resolving the Hett Situation

The GM should present the characters with at least three viable approaches, none of which is without cost:

**DESTROY HER:** Fight in daylight, when she is weakened but still dangerous (reduce to Moderate threat, DC 15, in the granary if approached carefully). Requires dealing a Critical injury and finishing it while she tries to flee into the grain. Her destruction is straightforward but violent, and some survivors — particularly Brother Aldous — will feel it is a tragedy rather than a victory.

**TRAP AND STARVE:** Seal the granary completely. Block the ventilation hatch, reinforce the door (**Crafting DC 13**), let her diminish. This takes days the characters may not have. She will grow quieter and eventually become torpid again. She will not die — vampires do not starve to death — but she will become inert enough to transport or ignore. Requires materials and time.

**THE BINDING:** Brother Aldous knows of an old faith working that can place a temporary compulsion on a newly turned vampire — a command to stay, to be still, to wait. It requires him to be within ten metres of the vampire (dangerous), costs him a Major faith working (**Severe Resolve loss, DC 18**), and only holds for a few days. Long enough for a relief column to arrive. But it will probably kill him — he is old, his Resolve is already depleted, and a Major working at his age and in his condition may not leave enough of him to recover. He will do it if asked. He will not tell the characters what it will cost him until after he has already paid it.

**FIND WHAT REMAINS:** A character with **Lore (DC 16)** or **Faith (DC 14)** may determine that newly turned vampires can sometimes be guided to a voluntary torpor if the human identity is sufficiently stimulated. This requires speaking to Hett — really speaking, not attacking — using her name, her husband's name, and specific memories. It is a social encounter against a near-feral creature with a Cunning of 10 and a Resolve of 8, which means the DC is achievable but the margin for error is small. Success means Hett enters torpor voluntarily. She can then be sealed in and left, or transported south in a sealed box to be dealt with by someone else. What "dealt with" eventually means is a question the adventure does not answer.

## ENCOUNTERS & CHALLENGES

### Scene 1 — The Road at Dusk

Arrival. The characters approach Harrow Cross along the mill road in failing light. This scene establishes atmosphere and gives tracking or survival characters something to find before the violence starts.

**`[THREAT: Easy | DC 12 | Reward: 5–10 XP]`**

A **Tracking (DC 12)** or **Wilderness Survival (DC 12)** roll reveals: the road has not been used by wheeled traffic in at least four days. Something has crossed it multiple times at the same point — north side of the crossroads, moving east then west then east again. The footprints are barefoot, human-sized, not deep enough for a living person's weight. They stop seemingly at random and resume from a different point, as though the creature lost the thread of what it was doing.

A **Lore (DC 14)** roll adds: this is consistent with recently turned undead in a disoriented state — specifically, the circling pattern reported near newly created vampires that have not yet developed the cunning to act with sustained purpose.

Characters who fail these rolls arrive at the mill without this foreknowledge and will have to piece it together under pressure later.

---

### Scene 2 — The Survivors

The negotiation at the mill door, and the subsequent conversation with the survivors. Social and information scene. No rolls required to enter.

**`[THREAT: Easy | DC 13 | Reward: 10 XP]`**

Emric will not share the full situation until he feels he can trust the characters. **Persuasion (DC 13)** or **Command (DC 13)** accelerates this. Failure means a longer, more cautious conversation that yields the same information eventually but delays the characters by half a night. Critical failure means Emric restricts their access to the building.

If the characters speak to **Nella** privately, she tells them three things without any roll required: the thing in the granary smells sweet, like fruit left too long; it sometimes stands at the granary door at night and makes sounds; and twice she has heard it say a single word. The word was *"Cale."* She did not know what it meant.

If the characters speak to **Brother Aldous**, he tells them what he knows about vampires and what he suspects about the old stone cross. He will not mention the binding working until directly asked whether anything can be done — and even then he will be oblique about the cost.

---

### Scene 3 — The Granary in Daylight

Investigating the granary while Hett is torpid. This is the characters' best window for non-violent options.

**`[THREAT: Moderate | DC 14 | Reward: 25 XP]`**

Moving through the granary without waking Hett requires **Stealth (DC 12)**. Failure: she stirs — roll 1d6: 1–3, she rises from the grain; 4–6, she shifts but does not emerge. Disturbing the mound directly, or making a loud noise within the granary, wakes Hett. In daylight she is weakened (reduce all her rolls by 2) but not incapacitated — she will flee toward the grain first, fight only if cornered. An escape attempt in daylight costs her a Minor injury per round outside the granary's shade.

**`[THREAT: Easy | DC 12 | Reward: 10 XP]`**

A **Tracking (DC 12)** roll in the granary tells the characters: this creature has been here for at least a week, returning to the same sleeping spot. Something is keeping it here — it could leave through the ventilation hatch if it tried, but it hasn't.

A **Lore (DC 14)** roll, examining the hatch: the iron fittings are old iron, not part of the original construction. Iron is a known suppressant of undead movement — not a reliable one, but enough that a confused newly turned vampire might perceive it as an obstacle that isn't there. She could leave. She doesn't know that.

---

### Scene 4 — Night Falls

This scene occurs whether or not the characters are ready for it. The sun goes down. The mill lantern will need oil by the end of this scene. There is none left in the building.

Hett emerges from the granary when the last daylight fades. The characters will hear the granary door swinging. If they are inside the mill, they will hear her begin to circle.

**`[THREAT: Moderate | DC 18 | Reward: 25–40 XP]`**

Any character who is outside after dark without precautions becomes a potential target.

**Precautions that work:** staying within fifteen metres of Brother Aldous (his faith aura suppresses her); carrying an active open flame (she will not approach); being actively armed and alert (she assesses the danger and avoids).

**Precautions that do not work:** blessed water has no effect on a vampire of this age; conventional holy symbols without an active faith user have no effect; silver is a fiction.

If a character is caught in the open without precautions, Hett will close distance. This triggers a combat encounter at **Hard difficulty (DC 20)**. The characters must then decide: fight, flee, or attempt contact.

---

### Scene 5 — The Climax

The climax occurs when one of three things happens: the characters have formulated a plan and execute it; a survivor attempts to flee independently and triggers an attack; or the characters run out of time (oil gone, night three, Brother Aldous's condition declining).

The GM should not force this — the climax should emerge from the characters' decisions. But it should not be delayed past the second night without cost.

See *Resolving the Hett Situation* above for the mechanical options. The most dramatically interesting outcome is usually the binding or the attempted dialogue — but the adventure does not privilege one outcome over another. A straightforward daylight fight that the characters survive is a legitimate ending.

### Hett — Additional GM Notes

Hett's husband Cale was left behind when Millbrook evacuated — he went back for their dog and the mob kept moving. He is alive, sheltering in Thornwall's lower garrison district. *"Cale"* is what she says when the hunger is worst and the human part of her surfaces.

If the characters somehow locate Cale and bring him to Harrow Cross — an event requiring significant effort and a separate journey — Hett's response to seeing him is the most powerful scene the adventure can produce. The GM should handle this with honesty: she will know what she is, and she will know what it would cost him to stay near her. What she asks for, or does not ask for, is left to the GM's judgment of what the characters and the story have earned.

---

### Adjusting Difficulty

**Easier (solo player or no combat skills):** Reduce Hett's Might by 2 in combat and have Brother Aldous be more forthcoming about the binding option earlier in the adventure.

**Harder (experienced players):** Add a second recently turned vampire — a farmhand from Millbrook who was turned the same night as Hett and has been sleeping in the ruins of a barn two hundred metres east of the crossroads. He has no name the characters will find. He is further along the transformation and will not respond to dialogue.

### Ending the Adventure

The adventure ends when the characters leave Harrow Cross — either escorting survivors south to Thornwall, having resolved the Hett situation one way or another, or having failed and retreated having done what they could.

There is no victory condition in the conventional sense. Brother Aldous may be dead. Hett may be destroyed, contained, or still somewhere out on the plains. The characters should leave Harrow Cross feeling the weight of what the plains cost — not triumphant, but perhaps a little more certain of who they are and what they are willing to pay.

That is the correct ending for an adventure in this world.
