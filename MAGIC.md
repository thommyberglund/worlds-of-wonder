
# MAGIC: BLOOD AND PRAYERS

Magic is not a gift. It is a debt, a wound, a whisper in the dark. Those who wield it are marked—by scars, by madness, by the things that watch from the corners of the world.

## CASTING SPELLS OR PRAYERS
1. **Declare Intent:** What do you want to achieve?
2. **GM Sets DC:** Based on the spell/prayer’s power and circumstances.
3. **Roll:**
   - **Arcane Magic:** Cunning + Arcana (if any) vs. DC.
   - **Faith Magic:** Resolve + Faith (if any) vs. DC.
4. **Pay the Cost:** HP, Resolve, a memory, or a divine test.
5. **Face the Consequences:** Corruption, divine wrath, or unintended effects.

## TYPES OF MAGIC
### Arcane
- **Blood Magic:** Fuelled by HP or others’ blood.
- **Pact Magic:** Bargains with entities.
- **Necromancy:** Fuelled by the dead.
- **Rune Magic:** Permanent tattoos or carvings.

### Faith
- **Prayers:** Petitions to gods.
- **Miracles:** Direct divine intervention.
- **Divine Marks:** Signs of a god’s favor or curse.

## LEARNING MAGIC
### Arcane
- **No Starting Magic:** Find grimoires, bargain with mentors, or steal power.
- **Advancing:** Unlock new corruption thresholds.

### Faith
- **Starting Faith:** Begin with Faith = Resolve modifier if you worship a god.
- **Advancing:** Increase Faith through acts of devotion.
- **Losing Faith:** Break vows or act against your god’s tenets.

## MAGIC IN THE WORLD
### Arcane
- **Who Uses It?** Sorcerers, witches, necromancers.
- **Where Is It Found?** Ruins, dying casters, cursed objects.

### Faith
- **Who Uses It?** Clerics, paladins, survivors.
- **Where Is It Found?** Temples, shrines, holy relics.

## GM TOOLS
- **Random Encounters:** Dying witches, holy relics, divine trials.
- **Settlement Reactions:** Magic is outlawed in Thornwall, tolerated in Saltmere, and silent in the Silent Cities.

## EXAMPLE SPELLS & PRAYERS
### Arcane
- **Minor:** Whisper, Candlelight.
- **Moderate:** Ghost Summon, Wound Knit.
- **Major:** Raise Dead, Demon Pact.
- **Forbidden:** Resurrection, Scry.

### Faith
- **Minor:** "Guide My Hand," "Bless This Meal."
- **Moderate:** "Heal This Wound," "Smite My Foe."
- **Major:** "Protect Us," "Banish the Dead."
- **Forbidden:** "Bring Him Back," "Show Me the Path."

## OPTIONAL RULES
- **Sanity Mechanic:** Track Sanity loss from magic and horrors.
- **Magic Items:** All are cursed or demand divine tests.

# NECROMANCY: THE ART OF THE UNQUIET DEAD

Necromancy is the most reviled form of magic, drawing power from the dead and the dying. Practitioners are hunted by the living and haunted by the dead.

## CASTING NECROMANTIC SPELLS
1. Declare intent.
2. GM sets DC based on the corpse/spirit’s freshness.
3. Roll Cunning + Necromancy vs. DC.
4. Pay the cost (HP, Resolve, or a memory).
5. Face the consequences: The dead remember you.

## NECROMANTIC CORRUPTION TABLE
| 1d6 | Effect                                  |
|-----|-----------------------------------------|
| 1   | Grave Touch (-1 Charisma).              |
| 2   | Whispers of the Dead (-1 Resolve).     |
| 3   | Rotting Fingers (cosmetic but noticeable). |
| 4   | Hollow Eyes (+1 Intimidation, -1 Diplomacy). |
| 5   | Bound Spirit (a ghost haunts you).      |
| 6   | Marked by Death (undead no longer attack you—but neither do the living trust you). |

## TYPES OF NECROMANTIC SPELLS
- **Minor:** Whisper to the Dead, Grave Light.
- **Moderate:** Animate Corpse, Steal Breath.
- **Major:** Raise Thrall, Bind Spirit.
- **Forbidden:** Resurrection, Necrotic Plague.

## THE DEAD REMEMBER
- Every corpse/spirit you use hunts you.
- Roll 1d6 after each spell:
  - 1–2: The dead hold a grudge.
  - 3–4: The dead whisper your name to others.
  - 5–6: The dead tell their kin (next necromancy DC +2).

## NECROMANCERS IN THE WORLD
- **Who Practices It?** Grave robbers, battlefield scavengers, cursed scholars.
- **Where Is It Found?** Battlefields, ruins, gallows.
- **Consequences:** The living fear you; the dead hunt you; the law condemns you.

# DIVINE MARKS: THE GODS’ CRUEL GIFTS

Divine Marks are the physical or spiritual signs of a god’s favor—or curse. They grant power but demand devotion, and breaking your vows invites divine wrath.

## ACQUIRING DIVINE MARKS
- **Acts of Devotion:** Perform a deed aligned with your god’s tenets.
- **Desperate Prayers:** Beg for aid in a moment of crisis.
- **Sacrifice:** Offer something precious (a limb, a memory, a loved one).

## DIVINE MARK TABLE
| Mark               | God’s Domain | Bonus                              | Restriction                          | Divine Wrath (if broken)          |
|--------------------|--------------|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Mark of the Martyr | Suffering    | +2 Resolve vs. pain.              | Cannot flee from combat.            | Lose 1d6 HP/day until atonement.  |
| Mark of the Judge  | Justice      | +1 to attack vs. the guilty.      | Must punish wrongdoers.             | Weapon turns against you.        |
| Mark of the Silent | Death        | Undead ignore you.                 | Cannot speak unless spoken to.      | Tongue rots.                      |
| Mark of the Beacon | Hope         | Allies gain +1 Resolve.           | Must aid the desperate.             | Light attracts monsters.         |
| Mark of the Chain  | Duty         | +1 Diplomacy with allies.          | Cannot abandon sworn tasks.        | Limbs stiffen (-1 Finesse).       |
| Mark of the Hollow | Sacrifice    | Ignore damage once/day.           | Must offer blood daily.             | Blood turns to dust.             |

## DIVINE MARKS IN DEPTH
- **Permanent:** Marks cannot be removed except by divine will.
- **Triggers:** Marks activate under specific conditions.
- **Divine Wrath:** Breaking your vows turns the Mark into a curse.

## DIVINE MARKS AND NECROMANCY
- **Conflict:** If you bear a Divine Mark and cast necromancy, roll 1d6:
  - 1–2: The Mark burns you (lose 1d4 HP).
  - 3–4: The dead fear your Mark (necromancy DC +2).
  - 5–6: Your god abandons you (lose the Mark’s bonus for 1d4 days).
