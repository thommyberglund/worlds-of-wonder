
# MAGIC: BLOOD AND PRAYERS

Magic is not a gift. It is a debt, a wound, a whisper in the dark. Those who wield it are marked—by scars, by madness, by the things that watch from the corners of the world.

## CASTING SPELLS OR PRAYERS
1. **Declare Intent:** What do you want to achieve?
2. **GM Sets DC:** Based on the spell/prayer’s power and circumstances.
3. **Roll:**
   - **Arcane Magic:** Cunning + Arcana (if any) vs. DC.
   - **Faith Magic:** Resolve + Faith (if any) vs. DC.
4. **Pay the Cost:** Arcane costs are torso injuries; faith costs are Resolve loss. See RULES.md (§ Pay the Cost) for the full tier table. Costs are mandatory and paid before the outcome is narrated.
5. **Face the Consequences:** Corruption, divine wrath, or unintended effects.

## SPELL/PRAYER TIERS
| Tier | Example Effects | Arcane Cost | Faith Cost | Corruption/Divine Test |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Minor | Light a candle | Minor torso injury | -1 Resolve | Veins darken / God demands a favor |
| Moderate | Summon a ghost | Moderate torso injury | -1d4 Resolve | Eyes glow / God tests your faith |
| Major | Raise the dead | Severe torso injury | -1d6 Resolve | Gain madness / God marks you |
| Forbidden | Resurrect the dead | Critical torso injury + permanent mark | Irreplaceable sacrifice | Roll twice on Corruption Table |

*Arcane injuries follow all normal injury rules and may trigger a Grit Check. A Forbidden spell's Critical torso injury requires stabilization (DC 20 Resolve) or death follows within the scene. Full cost rules in RULES.md (§ Pay the Cost).*

## TYPES OF MAGIC
### Arcane
- **Blood Magic:** Fuelled by HP or others’ blood.
- **Pact Magic:** Bargains with entities.
- **Necromancy:** Fuelled by the dead.
- **Rune Magic:** Permanent tattoos or carvings.

### Faith
- **Prayers:** Petitions to gods.
- **Miracles:** Direct divine intervention.
- **Divine Marks:** Signs of a god’s favor or curse.

## LEARNING MAGIC
### Arcane
- **No Starting Magic:** Find grimoires, bargain with mentors, or steal power.
- **Advancing:** Arcana advances through the standard skill tier system (Basic → Advanced → Mastery) at level-up. Advancing to Advanced tier triggers a Corruption roll. Arcana cannot be taken at character creation. Full rules in RULES.md (§ Special Skills).

### Faith
- **Starting Faith:** Begin with Faith = Resolve modifier if you worship a god. Declare your god and one vow at session zero.
- **Advancing:** Faith increases through acts of devotion and narrative milestones. The GM may award a tier increase for significant religious acts.
- **Losing Faith:** Break vows or act against your god’s tenets.

## MAGIC IN THE WORLD
### Arcane
- **Who Uses It?** Sorcerers, witches, necromancers.
- **Where Is It Found?** Ruins, dying casters, cursed objects.

### Faith
- **Who Uses It?** Clerics, paladins, survivors.
- **Where Is It Found?** Temples, shrines, holy relics.

## GM TOOLS
- **Random Encounters:** Dying witches, holy relics, divine trials.
- **Settlement Reactions:** Magic is outlawed in Thornwall, tolerated in Saltmere, and silent in the Silent Cities.

## EXAMPLE SPELLS & PRAYERS
### Arcane
- **Minor:** Whisper, Candlelight.
- **Moderate:** Ghost Summon, Wound Knit.
- **Major:** Raise Dead, Demon Pact.
- **Forbidden:** Resurrection, Scry.

### Faith
- **Minor:** "Guide My Hand," "Bless This Meal."
- **Moderate:** "Heal This Wound," "Smite My Foe."
- **Major:** "Protect Us," "Banish the Dead."

## OPTIONAL RULES
- **Sanity Mechanic:** Track Sanity loss from magic and horrors.
- **Magic Items:** All are cursed or demand divine tests.

# NECROMANCY: THE ART OF THE UNQUIET DEAD

Necromancy is the most reviled form of magic, drawing power from the dead and the dying. Practitioners are hunted by the living and haunted by the dead.

## CASTING NECROMANTIC SPELLS
1. Declare intent.
2. GM sets DC based on the corpse/spirit’s freshness.
3. Roll Cunning + Arcana bonus vs. DC. Necromancy is a subtype of arcane magic and uses the Arcana skill — not a separate Necromancy skill.
4. Pay the cost: same torso injury costs as all arcane magic. Additionally, each corpse or spirit used becomes aware of you.
5. Face the consequences: The dead remember you.

## NECROMANTIC CORRUPTION TABLE
| 1d6 | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Grave Touch (-1 to all Cunning-based social rolls; the smell of death clings to you) |
| 2 | Whispers of the Dead (-1 Resolve; the voices do not stop) |
| 3 | Rotting Fingers (cosmetic but noticeable; NPCs recoil from handshakes and contact) |
| 4 | Hollow Eyes (+1 Intimidation, -1 to Cunning-based social rolls) |
| 5 | Bound Spirit (a ghost haunts you; it is not friendly) |
| 6 | Marked by Death (undead no longer attack you — but neither do the living trust you) |

## TYPES OF NECROMANTIC SPELLS
- **Minor:** Whisper to the Dead, Grave Light.
- **Moderate:** Animate Corpse, Steal Breath.
- **Major:** Raise Thrall, Bind Spirit.
- **Forbidden:** Resurrection, Necrotic Plague.

## THE DEAD REMEMBER
- Every corpse/spirit you use hunts you.
- Roll 1d6 after each spell:
  - 1–2: The dead hold a grudge.
  - 3–4: The dead whisper your name to others.
  - 5–6: The dead tell their kin (next necromancy DC +2).

## NECROMANCERS IN THE WORLD
- **Who Practices It?** Grave robbers, battlefield scavengers, cursed scholars.
- **Where Is It Found?** Battlefields, ruins, gallows.
- **Consequences:** The living fear you; the dead hunt you; the law condemns you.

# DIVINE MARKS: THE GODS’ CRUEL GIFTS

Divine Marks are the physical or spiritual signs of a god’s favor—or curse. They grant power but demand devotion, and breaking your vows invites divine wrath.

## ACQUIRING DIVINE MARKS
- **Acts of Devotion:** Perform a deed aligned with your god’s tenets.
- **Desperate Prayers:** Beg for aid in a moment of crisis.
- **Sacrifice:** Offer something precious (a limb, a memory, a loved one).

## DIVINE MARK TABLE
| Mark | God's Domain | Bonus | Restriction | Divine Wrath (if broken) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mark of the Martyr | Suffering | +2 Resolve vs. pain | Cannot flee from combat | Suffer a Moderate torso injury each day until atonement |
| Mark of the Judge | Justice | +1 to Might or Finesse rolls vs. the guilty | Must punish wrongdoers | Weapon turns against you — -2 to all combat rolls |
| Mark of the Silent | Death | Undead ignore you | Cannot speak unless spoken to | Tongue rots — cannot speak at all until divine mercy |
| Mark of the Beacon | Hope | Allies gain +1 Resolve | Must aid the desperate | Light attracts monsters — encounters in darkness escalate one threat tier |
| Mark of the Chain | Duty | +1 to Cunning-based negotiation and persuasion | Cannot abandon sworn tasks | Limbs stiffen (-1 Finesse until atonement) |
| Mark of the Hollow | Sacrifice | Ignore damage once per day | Must offer blood daily | Blood turns to dust — cannot heal by any means until atonement |

## DIVINE MARKS IN DEPTH
- **Permanent:** Marks cannot be removed except by divine will.
- **Triggers:** Marks activate under specific conditions defined in the table above.
- **Divine Wrath:** Breaking your vows turns the Mark into a curse. Wrath effects persist until the character makes atonement — a narrative act determined by the GM that reflects the god's domain.
- **Marks and Divine Tests:** A character bearing a Divine Mark who triggers a Divine Test rolls on the standard table (RULES.md § Divine Tests) with one modification — on a result of 6 (Miracle), the mark itself is the vessel of that miracle, and the cost is taken from the mark's power rather than from an external sacrifice. The mark dims for 1d4 days afterward.

## DIVINE MARKS AND NECROMANCY
- **Conflict:** If you bear a Divine Mark and cast necromancy, roll 1d6:
  - 1–2: The Mark burns you — take a Minor torso injury in addition to the normal casting cost.
  - 3–4: The dead fear your Mark (necromancy DC +2).
  - 5–6: Your god abandons you (lose the Mark’s bonus for 1d4 days).
