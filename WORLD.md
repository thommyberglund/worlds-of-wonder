# CORE DESCRIPTION

- HIGH FANTASY WORLD: The world is a typical high fantsy setting with old mountains and spawling, dark forests. The old ruins and memories of old empires still linger in the corners of the world. The elves exist but are mostly found far away on islands in the vast ocean or deep in the forests. The Dwarfs have their mountain kingdoms and still trade with humans. Magic is powerful and vast with wizards in far away towers together with powerful necromancers in the deep hollows of the world. Shining knights, vast armies, dragons and treasures.

- GEOGRAPHY: It mimics earth climate types with cold northern areas covered with ice in the far north and a boreal forests further south. The central parts on the horizontal axis is made up of plains with a climate similar to southern france and north africa. Alternating between a few massive mountain ranges where forests still cling to the foot of the mountains and the vast plains where the current human population lives. The south is vast jungles and dry deserts. Old empires still display empty cities and humans live close to the seas in fortified cities.

- POPULATION: The proximity of the human population to the seas and the fortified nature of southern cities suggests a world where the wilderness is reclaiming the land, leaving civilization to cling to the safest borders. There are still villages further from the safe zones near the seas but they grow more infrequent and more fortified the further from the coast you travel.

# AREAS

## The Frozen North & Boreal Reaches
This is a land of silence and survival. The far north isn't just cold; it’s a graveyard of history.

- The Boreal Belt: Vast, dark evergreen forests where the Dwarven Mountain Kingdoms are most prominent. These fortresses are the lungs of the world's economy, exporting iron and enchanted stone.

- The Sylvan Deep: Within these forests, the few remaining Mainland Elves reside. Unlike their island cousins, these elves are guarded and fierce, protecting groves that predate the current human empires.

## The Central Plains: The Hearth of Humanity
Mimicking the warmth of Southern France and the arid beauty of North Africa, this is the stage for "shining knights and vast armies."

- The Feudal Corridors: Humanity has mastered the plains between the mountain ranges. Their cities are grand, but often built upon the foundations of things much older.

- The Wizard’s Spires: Isolated peaks rising from the plains house the Towers of High Magic. These wizards act as advisors or reclusive observers of the human wars below.

- The Necromantic Hollows: Beneath the feet of the galloping knights lie the "Deep Hollows"—ancient catacombs and fallen cities where necromancers sift through the bones of the old world to fuel their dark arts.

## The South: Jungles, Deserts, and Ghosts
The further south you go, the more the world feels "empty" of the living and "full" of the past.

- The Shattered Coast: Humans live in heavily fortified coastal cities, clinging to the sea for trade and escape routes. The interior is too dangerous to settle.

- The Silent Cities: Massive, sun-bleached ruins in the deserts and vine-choked stone ziggurats in the jungles. These are the domains of Dragons who hoard the gold of forgotten kings.

# HISTORY

## THE FIRST EMPIRES
- In the long forgotten past the first empires rose where there is now only desert. At the time it was a lush and comforting land with dark jungles and plenty of food. The old emperors soon discovered human sacrifice and the terrible powers it enabled. Soon the lands flowed red with blood and eldrich power crept into the world. The emperors because sorcerer-kings and enabled immortality at a terrible price. The life force of the land itself was consumed and soon all that was left were the vast sand dunes and the scorching sun. The immortal rulers themselves chose entombment to wait out the return of life to their ancient lands.

## THE ELVES
- While the sorcerer-kings sleep and wait for the world to be anew the first elves arrived. They brought magic with them of a different kind where they sought to stop the natural change of the world. Static beauty and never ending thoughts of peace. Legend say they arrived en masse together with the three moons that can be seen on cloudless nights. They don't belong of this world and old tales tell of how they first were cruel and careless. Casuallt slaughtering the orks and goblins that were the original inhabitants of the plains and forests. But something changed. The tales tell of a civil was of a magnitude never seen since then. The empire was utterly raised to the ground and the static glades where spirits were prepared to meet eternity was gone forever. Left was the elves we still meet today. Those that hid in the deep forests and far away on remote islands. They still guard their secrets and dream of eternal calm.

## THE FIRST MEN
- Ages came and went and the world was silent. The orks and goblins once again populated the lands and mountains. The dwarfs slowly made their presence known. Where they came from is still unclear and their elders claim to have no knowledge of their origins.
- From the east arrived the first tribes of men across a land bridge that has since then been sunk under the sea. They were the Kwa-Hazalits. They already knew how to form the copper and bronze needed for tools and armour and they soon learned to forge iron and steel from the dwarfs. They brought with them something else than magic. The Fate. A divine belief in their god that empowers the people and give blessings to those who believe.
- THE NEW EMPIRE: Soon more tribes followed. They shared the same god as the Kwa-Hazalits but names and practices were different. One tribe founded a village at the central plains and started farming. Soon the village grew and towns followed. Advance the time 4000 years and the small village is now the empire of of the sun.
- THE DECAY: The borders of the empire now slowly expands and meet the old and eldrich ruins to the south. What was once desert have now receeded and new life has started to grow. But the old powers are still there. The first human necromancers rise. Soon follow armies of the undead. Strange rites awake the sleeping sorcerer-kings closest minions. They arrive back in the world as strange undead creatures that can only receive nurture from the blood of the living. The are named vampires. Soon the empire is on the retreat and after years of brutal fighting the capital falls and is overrun by the undead.
- THE END: Pressed against the rim of the mountains the few remaining priests have little choice. They enact a rite only thought of a a last stand. The stories are unclear about what happends next. Some clain the soul of every believer within a 1000 kilometers is absorbed and the living embodiment of their god manifests. Others claim that the sun stops and day never ends. Vampires and undead alike evaporates in the holy radiance of the sun and only after 10 years of eternal day night finally arrives.
- THE RESULT: Not a single human is left between the mountains and the warm sea. The few surviving villages have already received massive amounts of refugees and soon grow to regular towns and cities. The central plains is considered off limit and only after 800 years the first colonists start to creep back into the plains.
- TODAY: The recolonization of the old empire is slow. The undead never really went away and many still roam parts of the plains. Orks and goblins have reclaimed some of the areas and the few hardy villages battle orks during the day and undead during the night. The coastal cities and grand and powerful. They have formed kingdoms and huge armies are mustering to once and for all reclaim the heartlands of humanity.

## PRESENT WORLD NOTES
- The presence of the "Waiting" Sorcerer-Kings provides a ticking clock. If the deserts begin to bloom again, it might not be a blessin, it might be the dinner bell for the First Emperors.

- The Elven Paradox: The Elves' desire for "static peace" may be the only thing keeping the world's natural cycles slow enough to keep the Kings asleep—or their magic may be exactly what the Kings need to wake up.

- The Fate vs. Magic: While Wizards study the mechanics of the world, those who follow The Fate manifest power through sheer belief, creating a cultural tension between the learned and the devout.

- The Iron Pact: A merchant association operating across the Shattered Coast cities, nominally a trade consortium but in practice a network of commercial interests that operates in the space between the church, the nobility, and the harbour councils. The Iron Pact does not hold territory or field armies — its power is debt, contract, and information. In smaller settlements like Thistle Hold, it may be the only organised authority that functions day to day. It is not evil in any simple sense. It is interested, which is often more dangerous.
