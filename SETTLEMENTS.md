# SETTLEMENTS
## World Geography Reference — Chronicle of the Central Plains

---

### Coordinate System

This world uses a 1000×1000 grid mapped to Earth climate analogs. The coordinate system runs:

- **X axis (West → East):** 0 to 1000
- **Y axis (North → South):** 0 to 1000

#### Climate Bands by Y coordinate

| Y Range | Zone |
|---|---|
| 0–150 | Frozen North / polar ice |
| 151–300 | Boreal Belt — dark evergreen forests, Dwarven kingdoms, Mainland Elven groves |
| 301–500 | Northern Plains transition — temperate, forested foothills |
| 501–650 | Central Plains — Southern France / North Africa analog, Hearth of Humanity |
| 651–750 | Southern Plains frontier — recolonization zone, undead-haunted |
| 751–850 | The Shattered Coast — fortified coastal cities, warm sea |
| 851–1000 | Deep South — jungles, deserts, silent cities, dragon territories |

#### Major Geographic Features

- **Western Ocean:** X=0–150 (open sea, elven island chains)
- **Western Mountain Range:** approximately X=180–220, running Y=150–700
- **Central Plains:** X=220–780, Y=501–750
- **Eastern Mountain Range:** approximately X=780–820, running Y=150–700
- **Warm Southern Sea:** Y=820–850 (coastal band)
- **Deep Southern Desert:** X=250–750, Y=880–1000
- **Deep Southern Jungle:** X=150–850, Y=860–950 (flanking the desert)

## Settlements — Active or Recently Active

---

### Ashford
**Coordinates:** X=520, Y=700
**Type:** Frontier village (destroyed)
**Population:** ~43 (before the attack; 14 survivors)
**Status:** Abandoned / overrun

**Description:** A small colonial settlement on the open central plains, three seasons old at the time of the story. Built in the slow recolonization zone where the old empire's heartland is being cautiously resettled. Surrounded by flat grassland with no natural defenses beyond a timber palisade. The nearest village is several hours' travel in any direction.

Ashford sat at the edge of what locals call the safe zone — close enough to the mill road to receive supplies from the north, far enough into the plains that the dead were a nightly concern. It was overrun by six undead in a single night, its survivors fleeing north along the mill road to Henric's Mill.

**Terrain:** Open plains, yellow grass, flat. Timber palisade wall. Single well. Gatehouse with torch post.

**Significance:** Starting location of Chapter One. Origin of the refugees at Henric's Mill.

---

### Henric's Mill
**Coordinates:** X=510, Y=680
**Type:** Working mill / emergency refuge
**Population:** ~8 permanent; 14 Ashford refugees sheltering
**Status:** Active

**Description:** A mill two leagues north of Ashford along the mill road, positioned where a reliable stream provides water power. Old Henric operated it before the recolonization wave and stayed when others left, giving the mill a permanence that newer settlements lack. The building is solid stone on the ground floor, timber above, with a defensible interior and a heavy door.

When Ashford fell, the miller led twelve survivors north along the mill road and fortified the mill as an emergency refuge. The mill's lantern served as a beacon visible from the road.

**Terrain:** Rolling plains, one stream, a stand of birch to the east. Mill road runs north-south past the front.

**Significance:** Refuge for Ashford survivors. Contract origin point — Osbert hired Aldric to reach Thornwall for 40 silver marks. Bram rode here at end of Chapter One to deliver news of the road.

---

### Crale's Ford
**Coordinates:** X=520, Y=730
**Type:** Frontier village (abandoned)
**Population:** 0 (evacuated)
**Status:** Empty but intact

**Description:** A village of approximately thirty souls, positioned where the mill road crosses a shallow ford in a seasonal river. The name suggests it was established by a founding family. When the undead threat spread south, the entire population organized and fled toward the coast in good order — doors left open, a pot still hanging over a cold hearth, chickens wandering the street. No violence here. Just absence.

**Terrain:** Shallow river crossing, ford with flat stones. Open plains on all sides. A dozen timber-and-thatch buildings around a central well.

**Significance:** First village south of Henric's Mill. Confirmed that the threat was moving fast enough to prompt organized evacuation rather than a last stand.

---

### Dunmore
**Coordinates:** X=520, Y=750
**Type:** Village (cleared)
**Population:** ~30 (evacuated or dead); Bram the only survivor recovered
**Status:** Cleared of undead; functionally abandoned

**Description:** A village of eight buildings around a central well, positioned on the coast road between the frontier and Thornwall. The inn — the tallest building, two storeys — gave Bram a refuge when the village fell. The granary on the east side provided emergency shelter for children who arrived later.

Dunmore was hit harder than Crale's Ford. Six undead moved through it, killing or turning several residents before the survivors fled south. The eastern granary was where Ashford's children sheltered the night before Aldric arrived — they were directed north to Henric's Mill the following morning.

**Terrain:** Flat plains, coast road running north-south, low scrub to the east. Central well. Inn, granary, six smaller buildings.

**Significance:** Location of Bram's rescue. Six undead cleared by Aldric. Children directed to Henric's Mill. Bram joined the party here.

---

### Harrow Cross
**Coordinates:** X=510, Y=770
**Type:** Village (abandoned)
**Population:** 0 (fled south); children sheltering in granary at time of passage
**Status:** Abandoned; eastern granary barred from inside at time of visit

**Description:** A village at a crossroads on the coast road, notable for the ruins of an old stone cross that gives it its name — a religious marker from an earlier period of settlement, the faith unclear. The eastern granary burned during the flight, its roof collapsing, though the main buildings survived. When Aldric passed through, the adult population had already fled south to Thornwall. A group of children had barred themselves in the surviving granary.

**Terrain:** Crossroads. Open plains, low hills visible to the east. Collapsed granary on eastern edge. Smoke from the burned granary roof visible for several kilometers.

**Significance:** Aldric directed sheltering children north toward Henric's Mill. Confirmed civilian evacuation toward Thornwall was underway. The troll was encountered on the road approaching from the north.

---

### Thornwall
**Coordinates:** X=500, Y=800
**Type:** Major fortified city
**Population:** ~8,000
**Status:** Active; merchant quarter breach resolved at end of Chapter One

**Description:** One of the grand coastal cities of the Shattered Coast, built where a rocky headland provides natural defense on two sides and a deep harbor on the third. The city walls are forty feet of worked stone, maintained and garrisoned by the Lord Commander's forces. Thornwall serves as the primary supply and staging point for the recolonization effort pushing north into the central plains.

The city is organized in distinct districts: the garrison district near the south gate, the merchant quarter in the city's center, the scholars' quarter to the north, the harbor district to the east, and the working harbor facing the warm sea.

The merchant quarter was breached by the First Warden during the events of Chapter One, resulting in eleven missing residents, multiple deaths, and a three-day containment operation. The breach was resolved when Aldric performed the binding renewal beneath the trading hall well.

**Notable locations within Thornwall:**

| Location | Description |
|---|---|
| The South Gate | Primary land entrance; sealed during the crisis |
| The Garrison Annex | Captain Undh's base of operations |
| The Merchant Quarter | Site of the Warden breach; sealed and then cleared |
| The Trading Hall | Built over the hollow point containing the First Warden's binding well |
| The Scholars' Quarter | Archive district; Pol Edwick's former rooms located here |
| The Old Harbor | Silted crescent behind the working harbor; site of the Verath tower |
| The Verath Tower | Ancient stone tower, four storeys, built into the old harbor seawall; site of the interrupted binding renewal |
| Mira Solv's Apothecary | Apothecary lane; physician who treated Aldric's wrist |

**Terrain:** Rocky coastal headland. Stone walls. Deep harbor. Warm sea to the south.

**Significance:** Primary city of Chapter One. Lord Commander's seat. Destination of Aldric's contracted journey. Site of the Warden crisis and its resolution.

---

### Saltmere
**Coordinates:** X=480, Y=810
**Type:** Fortified coastal city (mid-sized)
**Population:** ~4,000 (estimated)
**Status:** Active; receiving refugees from the north

**Description:** A coastal city one day's ride southwest of Thornwall along the coast road, positioned on a lower headland with a working harbor and functional walls. Smaller than Thornwall but well-maintained, with an active garrison under a commander who received Undh's letter of introduction at the start of Chapter Two.

Saltmere serves as a secondary supply hub and refugee processing point. Its harbor is busy with incoming vessels and its streets show the characteristic quiet tension of a city absorbing more people than it expected while watching its northern roads with increasing alarm.

**Terrain:** Low headland. Coast road approach from the northeast. Harbor facing south. Lower walls than Thornwall but in better repair in places.

**Significance:** Destination at the start of Chapter Two. Aldric carries Undh's letter of introduction to the garrison commander. Dara Voss was en route here when attacked. The stolen Verath letter was addressed to Saltmere's harbormaster.

## Settlements — Referenced but Unvisited

---

### The Capital (fallen)
**Coordinates:** X=550, Y=600 (estimated)
**Type:** Ruined imperial capital
**Population:** 0 living; unknown undead presence
**Status:** Destroyed / haunted

**Description:** The seat of the Empire of the Sun, founded on a small village that grew over four thousand years into the greatest human city of its age. It fell to the undead armies when vampires drove the empire back from its southern borders and the capital was overrun before the last stand of the priesthood.

Its ruins stand in the central plains, a city of grand boulevards and imperial architecture slowly being reclaimed by grass and silence. The undead still walk its streets.

**Terrain:** Central plains. Estimated to be built on a river confluence, now silted. Surrounding flatlands.

---

### The Silent Cities (collective)
**Coordinates:** X=400–700, Y=880–980 (spread across the deep south)
**Type:** Ruins of the First Empire
**Population:** 0 living; dragons; entombed sorcerer-kings
**Status:** Ancient ruins; extremely dangerous

**Description:** The remains of the first human empire, predating the Empire of the Sun by thousands of years. Once built in a lush jungle-and-river landscape, the sorcerer-kings' blood rites consumed the land's life force until only desert remained. The cities stand half-buried in sand, their architecture vast and inhuman, their treasuries plundered only at the edges — the deep vaults remain unbreached.

Dragons nest in the outer structures, hoarding the gold of forgotten kings. The sorcerer-kings themselves lie entombed in the deepest chambers, waiting for the desert to bloom again.

**Terrain:** Varies — deep desert, encroaching jungle, crumbling coastline. All hostile.

---

### Dwarven Mountain Kingdoms (collective)
**Coordinates:** X=150–220, Y=200–700 (western range); X=780–850, Y=200–700 (eastern range)
**Type:** Fortified mountain kingdoms
**Population:** Unknown; significant
**Status:** Active; trading with human cities

**Description:** The dwarven kingdoms carve their halls into the two great mountain ranges that flank the central plains. Their origins are unknown even to their own elders. They export iron, enchanted stone, and worked metal through trade routes connecting to the coastal cities. Their mountain fortresses are the lungs of the world's economy.

---

### Elven Territories — Sylvan Deep
**Coordinates:** X=250–450, Y=220–350 (scattered groves within the boreal belt)
**Type:** Ancient forest groves
**Population:** Unknown; small and secretive
**Status:** Active but isolationist

**Description:** The remaining mainland elves protect ancient groves within the boreal forests — groves that predate the current human empires. Fierce and guarded, they allow no outsiders. Their magic works to preserve and maintain static states: unchanged forests, unchanged seasons, unchanged time.

---

### Elven Island Territories
**Coordinates:** X=50–200, Y=500–700 (scattered islands in the western ocean)
**Type:** Remote island settlements
**Population:** Unknown
**Status:** Active but unreachable to most

**Description:** The majority of the world's elven population withdrew to remote ocean islands after the great civil war that destroyed their empire. They are rarely seen on the mainland.
